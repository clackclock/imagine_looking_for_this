Pre-Interview Questions

Social media was seemingly built from ill intentions. The main factors that are negatively affecting people are the subtle addictive qualities that gradually take over a person's day to day life, and the unhealthy environment being produced by the big business's manufacturing and nurturing the worst in others.

Fixing social media would need the businesses in control to re-wire their way of thinking about their consumers. Personally I would say it's mostly impossible only because it hard to remove the "must earn money" mentality from big businesses that want to get bigger, but a possible solution would be removing the algorithms based around learning about a person and the "pay to win" features most social apps have. These features are usually the go to example when talking about how something has created a toxic environment for consumers. For example, Instagram sets itself up to send photos and reels that appeal to the user to the user's opening page and advertises and encourages users to pay to "boost" or promote their posts for the illusion of gaining more positive feedback through likes. And in recent updates photos have been nearly nonexistance with the boost in popularity to short form video posts, which has caused many content creators on the platform to start posting videos of their pictures to get any traction at all, which in a lot of cases leads to a downward spiral with unmotivation leading to demoralization, depression, etc, until eventually they either stop using the app or use to channel the built up bitter emotions.

It is instances like this that make me think that social media is impossible to reform, but the reason I said "mostly" above is because I have seen platforms that have removed these features. Most are privately owned like the Special.fish, but I have seem some mainstream platforms almost achieve this before being taken over. A personal favorite is Tumblr, which is basically a blog forum site. It has a for-you page like most social media where things can be suggested to you, but for the most part this page is made for you and by you from your own input. A few years ago the site was bought out by Yahoo, but for the most part the app has maintained most of it's charm of not actively trying to brainwash it's users and making fun of mainstream social media features, like people paying for Twitter checkmarks. And the community is mostly made up of people who work to keep the environment as non-toxic as possible and maintain the charming aspects I mentioned. Overall, I believe that social media is something can be reformed for people and society to enjoy and benefit from if the platform can be allowed to exist without big business input and given a chance to grow with a community that likes it's features and actually wants to work together to maintain itself instead of feeding the machine of bad ideas and "pay to win" functions.

Post Interview Questions

I believe in Jaron's argument because I've seen so many examples of what he's talking about. I've seen the depressive spirals people manage to get into and the inexplicable addictions to intangible feedback and people. I've lived through elections and presidentcies that have been dramatically effected by anonoymous commenters. Something's going to give. However I can confidently say something is being done about it, because all of us have seen the reports from people talking about how unhealthy social media is, and the alternative posting websites people have made to alleviate the stress of big business social medias and the small changes that get passed when enough people ban together to make a difference. I think if there going to be any lasting change it is going to be through a community effort, and it has to be otherwise nothing will get done.
