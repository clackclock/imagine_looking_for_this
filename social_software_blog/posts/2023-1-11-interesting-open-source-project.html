What is the software? What does it do and who does it serve?

Created by the gitHub user LucasHazardous and one other official contributor, this software is basically a non-profit casino that focuses on intuitive casino type games and less on money making. There is in-game "currency" that gets linked to the user's browser but the project's main purpose is to "give you an insight why you should never think of a casino as a way to make money".

This online casino serves people who enjoy playing casino games without the thrills and risks of chasing a reward. I started looking deeper into it because of how chill it sounded. While I enjoy the risk-reward stress system of traditional casinos, I do see the appeal in calmly enjoying the games without having to risk your entire pay-check and/or life savings.

What is the license for the software? What is expected of contributors?

They use the GNU General Public License v3.0. Basically they belive in the copyLeft philosophy of "you can use my open source code and distribute it freely at your leisure, but you must provide the same right and license to everyone after you". The "everyone after" being contributors, who seem to be randoms who joined to add their own games or modifications. The long and the short of contributing to this project seems to be; be respectful and if you want to add something to the official release make sure you have an understanding of the languages being used and are able to provide detailed descriptions of what it being changed with your updates.

The project itself seems to be towards the end of it's life-cycle. With the most recent update being a bug fix 8 months ago I would say the project is wrapping up. While I was playing on the site I noticed some placed where it could use adjustments and funny bugs. However, since it's purpose is to serve as a template and example of no-reward based gambling for what the site looks like and is able to do right now it can be considered complete.

What kind of help do they need?

If I were to contribute, it would probably be to just add more games or graphics. Looking at their project as a whole they don't really need more help outside of bug fixes and maybe some "quality of life" or accessibility changes.
